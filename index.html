Elon Musk

Elon Musk is the founder of several companies and is currently the world's richest person, with a net worth of over $413 billion. His wealth is primarily tied to his ownership stakes in Tesla and SpaceX. His career began with a series of successful startups: he co-founded Zip2, an online city guide, and then X.com, which became PayPal. After the sale of PayPal, he founded SpaceX in 2002 to pioneer reusable rockets and commercial spaceflight, with the long-term goal of colonizing Mars. He then joined Tesla as a major investor in 2004 and became CEO in 2008, leading the company to dominate the electric vehicle market. His other ventures include Neuralink, focusing on brain-computer interfaces, The Boring Company, for tunnel-based transportation, and xAI, his latest AI company that acquired X (formerly Twitter).

Alexander Wang

Alexander Wang is a prominent American entrepreneur and the co-founder of Scale AI, a company that provides data labeling and model evaluation for AI development. Born in Los Alamos, New Mexico, to physicists, he showed an early talent for computer science. Wang dropped out of MIT at 19 to co-found Scale AI in 2016. Under his leadership, the company quickly became a crucial partner for businesses and governments working with artificial intelligence. His success led him to become the world's youngest self-made billionaire at age 24. He stepped down as CEO of Scale AI in 2025 to join Meta but remains on the company's board. Chatgpt

Founded in 2015 as a nonprofit by Sam Altman and Elon Musk, OpenAI's initial mission was to ensure artificial general intelligence (AGI) benefits all of humanity. The company focused on open research, releasing tools like OpenAI Gym and training an AI to play Dota 2.
In 2019, OpenAI shifted to a "capped-profit" model, securing billions in investment from Microsoft to fund massive AI training. This led to the development of the groundbreaking GPT language models. The release of GPT-3 in 2020 showcased its ability to generate human-like text, setting the stage for its most famous creation.
The company's history culminated in the viral launch of ChatGPT in November 2022, which brought conversational AI to the mainstream. This success was followed by the release of more advanced models, including the multimodal GPT-4 and the latest GPT-5 in August 2025. This rapid evolution cemented OpenAI's status as a leading AI company, though it has also faced internal conflict and public debate over its direction and safety.

Sam Altman

Sam Altman is a key figure in the AI industry, known for his leadership at OpenAI, the company behind ChatGPT. He dropped out of Stanford to co-found his first company, Loopt. He later became president of Y Combinator, a prominent startup accelerator, before taking the helm at OpenAI in 2019. His career is marked by a focus on technology that benefits humanity and his influence has made him a leading voice in the conversation about AI's future.
